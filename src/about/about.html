About ApexSense

ApexSense is a desktop overlay suite designed for iRacing. It gives you real-time access to live tire temperatures, tire wear, tire pressure, a proximity radar, a live standings board, and an AI-assisted setup coach that analyses your lap telemetry and recommends setup changes.

It's built to be simple, useful, and readable while you're racing — no clutter, no learning curve. Just the data you need, right where you need it.

Features
◉

Live Tire Telemetry

Outside, middle, and inside temperatures for each tire with wear % and pressure, colour-coded cold→ideal→overheat in real time.

◎

Proximity Radar

Top-down radar showing nearby cars with distance-based colour coding. Configurable range, scale, car dimensions and opacity.

☰

Live Standings Board

Floating standings overlay showing all drivers with position, country flag, car number, iRating, safety rating, best/last lap times and incident count. Groups cars by class automatically in multi-class races.

◑

Setup Coach

Record a lap, describe your handling problem, and the coach analyses your telemetry — throttle, brake, tyre temps — then gives targeted suspension, aero and damper recommendations with an exportable setup file.

⚙

Adjustable Settings

Scale, opacity, thresholds, polling rate, column visibility and car sizes — all individually configurable per overlay.

◈

Persistent Layout

Window positions and sizes are saved automatically. The standings board is freely resizable. Everything is exactly where you left it next time you launch.

FAQ
ApexSense is an independent desktop overlay suite for iRacing. It includes a live tire telemetry overlay, a proximity radar, a floating standings board, and an AI-assisted setup coach — all running as transparent overlays on top of the game.
Yes — all overlays sit on top of iRacing in real time. They update continuously as you drive, showing live tire data, nearby traffic, race standings, and more.
Each tire shows three temperature zones (outside, middle, inside), average tire wear percentage, and current tire pressure. Each zone is individually colour-coded cold → warming → ideal → hot → overheat based on thresholds you set in the launcher.
The radar is a top-down view centred on your car. Nearby cars appear as coloured rectangles — red for very close (<5 m), yellow for caution (5–15 m), green for safe distance (>15 m). Car dimensions, radar range, scale, and opacity are all adjustable from the Radar tab.
The standings board is a floating, scrollable overlay showing every driver in the session with their position, country flag, car number, car model, iRating, safety rating, best lap, last lap, and incident count. You can toggle individual columns on/off both from the launcher and directly inside the overlay itself. In multi-class races it automatically groups drivers by car class with a coloured class header. The board is freely resizable and its size is remembered between sessions.
Open the Coach tab, import your current iRacing setup file (Step 1), select a handling problem like understeer, oversteer, or braking instability (Step 2), then record a lap while driving (Step 3). The coach analyses your throttle, braking, steering, and tyre temperature telemetry and generates specific setup recommendations — spring rates, damper settings, aero balance, tyre pressures — tailored to your car and problem (Step 4). You can then export a modified setup file ready to load in iRacing (Step 5), and review past sessions in History.
Yes. When overlays are unlocked, you can drag each one to any position on screen. The standings board is also resizable. All positions and the standings size are saved automatically between sessions.
ApexSense runs on your desktop monitor. You'll be able to see it on your screen, but it won't appear inside the VR headset by default. To bring it into VR, you can use a VR overlay tool like OVR Toolkit or similar, depending on your setup.
No. ApexSense is fully self-contained — just run the exe. No Node.js, Python, or extra runtimes needed. Everything is bundled inside the app.
Polling interval controls how often ApexSense refreshes telemetry data from iRacing. Lower values (50–100 ms) update more often and feel smoother but use slightly more CPU. Higher values (200+ ms) are lighter on resources. The default (200 ms) works well for most setups.
Yes. The Tires tab in the launcher lets you set four temperature thresholds that control when zone colours change: cold → warming → ideal → hot → overheat. Adjust them to match the tyre compound or car you're driving.
